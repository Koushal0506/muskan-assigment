Planck's Constant Experiment

Determining Planck's constant using LEDs of different colors

Start Experiment Learn Theory

Objective

To determine Planck's constant by measuring the threshold voltage of LEDs of different colors and analyzing the relationship between voltage and wavelength.

Apparatus Required

LEDs of different colors

DC Power Supply

Voltmeter (0-20V)

Ammeter (0-200mA)

Connecting Wires

Planck's Constant Setup

Theory

Planck's constant (h) is a fundamental physical constant that relates the energy of a photon to its frequency. The energy (E) of a photon is given by:

E = hν = hc/λ

where ν is the frequency, c is the speed of light, and λ is the wavelength of the photon.

In an LED, when electrons recombine with holes across the band gap, they emit photons with energy approximately equal to the band gap energy (Eg). The threshold voltage (Vth) at which an LED starts emitting light is related to the band gap energy by:

Eg ≈ eVth

where e is the electronic charge. Combining these equations, we get:

eVth ≈ hc/λ

By measuring the threshold voltage for LEDs of different wavelengths, we can determine Planck's constant from the slope of a plot of Vth versus 1/λ.

Procedure

  1. Set up the circuit as shown in the diagram, connecting the power supply, voltmeter, ammeter, and LED.
  2. Start with the red LED and set the voltmeter to the 20V range and ammeter to the 200mA range.
  3. Switch on the power supply and gradually increase the voltage in steps of 0.1V.
  4. Observe the ammeter reading. When the LED just starts to glow, note the voltage (threshold voltage).
  5. Record the corresponding current reading.
  6. Repeat the procedure for LEDs of different colors (blue, green, yellow, orange).
  7. Tabulate the readings of threshold voltage versus wavelength for each LED.
  8. Plot a graph of threshold voltage (Vth) versus the reciprocal of wavelength (1/λ).
  9. Determine Planck's constant from the slope of the graph using the relation h = (slope × e)/c.

Precautions

  • Ensure all connections are tight and correct before switching on the power supply.
  • Increase the voltage very slowly near the threshold voltage to get an accurate reading.
  • Do not exceed the maximum current rating of the LEDs to prevent damage.
  • Take readings in a dimly lit room to clearly observe when the LED starts glowing.
  • Use fresh batteries or a stable power supply to ensure consistent voltage readings.

Viva Questions

Planck's constant is a fundamental constant in quantum mechanics that relates the energy of a photon to its frequency. It sets the scale of quantum effects and appears in many fundamental equations of quantum theory.

Different colored LEDs emit light of different wavelengths, which corresponds to different photon energies. The threshold voltage is directly related to the energy band gap of the semiconductor material used in the LED, which determines the color of light emitted.

Possible sources of error include:

  • Difficulty in precisely determining the exact threshold voltage when the LED just starts glowing
  • Variation in LED characteristics due to manufacturing tolerances
  • Temperature dependence of the LED characteristics
  • Instrumental errors in voltage and current measurements
